What Are Optical Illusions?

An optical illusion is a visual phenomenon where the brain perceives something differently from reality. These illusions occur due to how our brain processes visual information.

Types of Optical Illusions

  • Literal Illusions: When the brain perceives an image that is different from the physical reality.
  • Physiological Illusions: Created by overstimulation of the brain with patterns, colors, or brightness.
  • Cognitive Illusions: When our perception is influenced by expectations and prior knowledge.
